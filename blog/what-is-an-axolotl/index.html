An axolotl (say "AX-oh-LOT-ul") is a type of salamander that lives underwater its entire life. Unlike most salamanders, axolotls never go through metamorphosis — they stay in their cute baby form forever! Scientists call this neoteny.

They come from one single place on Earth: Lake Xochimilco, a network of canals and lakes in Mexico City, Mexico. The Aztecs named them after their god Xolotl, who legends say disguised himself as a salamander to escape danger.
